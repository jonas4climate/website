My Master's thesis, at Forschungszentrum Jülich (Institute of Climate and Energy
Systems, ICE-1), tackled a practical barrier to better building control: advanced
controllers need detailed models, and building models are costly to build one at
a time.

## What I built

- A **toolkit for scalable, automated generation** of high-order, multi-zone
  building floor models — so complex control solutions become cost-effective to
  deploy across existing buildings, not just a single showcase.
- A **multi-agent system (MAS)** to test control strategies and assess their
  impact on the flexibility of multi-zone building systems.

## Why flexibility

Buildings that can shift *when* they use energy are a resource for a renewable
grid. Using the generated models, I studied how much demand-side flexibility
multi-zone buildings offer and how different control strategies unlock it.
