Solving classic partial differential equations numerically (wave, diffusion,
Laplace, reaction–diffusion) and using
[diffusion-limited aggregation](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/mathematics/diffusion-limited-aggregation)
to model the fractal growth of coral.

## Solving classic PDEs

Finite-difference schemes for the time-dependent wave and diffusion equations,
and iterative solvers (Jacobi, Gauss–Seidel, SOR) for the steady-state Laplace
equation.

## Diffusion-limited aggregation — growing coral

The headline model: random-walking particles that stick on contact grow a
branching, self-similar fractal — a simple mechanism for coral-like structures.

## Eigenmodes of a vibrating membrane

Discretising the Helmholtz eigenvalue problem turns a "drum" into a sparse
matrix whose eigenvectors are its vibrational modes.
